High intelligence is often seen as the ultimate cognitive advantage, a key to success and a shield against the complexities of the world. We celebrate geniuses and prodigies, assuming their powerful minds grant them a smoother path through life. But what if, beyond a certain threshold, extreme intelligence becomes less of a gift and more of a burden? What if the same mental machinery that allows for profound insight also creates a unique and silent form of suffering? This is the paradox of high intelligence—an exploration into the hidden complexities and distortions that can accompany a powerful intellect.
