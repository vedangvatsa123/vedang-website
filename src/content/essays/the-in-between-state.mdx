---
title: "The In-Between State"
summary: "Exploring transhumanism's awkward adolescence: the decades where we're enhanced but not transformed, stuck between human and post-human."
date: "2024-08-01"
---

The grand narratives of transhumanism are stories of endpoints. They paint a picture of a glorious, post-human future where we have uploaded our consciousness to the cloud, merged with machines to become godlike beings, or conquered aging and death itself. These are powerful, compelling visions. They are also a form of science fiction. The reality of our transhuman future will not be a sudden, glorious transformation. It will be a long, awkward, and often painful adolescence. We are entering the In-Between State, a multi-decade-long purgatory where we are enhanced but not transformed, augmented but not transcendent, stuck in the messy, uncomfortable space between human and post-human.

This is not a future of sleek, perfectly integrated cybernetics. It’s a future of glitchy firmware, of compatibility issues between your neural implant and your smart home, of subscription fees for your own memories. It's a world where your cognitive enhancements are ad-supported, and your new bio-printed liver is only warrantied for five years. The In-Between State is where the sublime vision of transhumanism collides with the messy reality of market forces, planned obsolescence, and buggy version 1.0 hardware.

Consider the first generation of widely available cognitive enhancements. They won't grant us genius-level intelligence. They’ll be more like a permanent, low-grade Adderall drip, a BCI that helps you focus by subtly filtering your sensory input and suppressing distracting thoughts. It will offer a real, tangible advantage, but it will also come with side effects. Maybe it dampens your creativity, or flattens your emotional range. Maybe the long-term effects on neural plasticity are unknown. Users will be the beta testers for their own evolution.

Or think about life extension. We won't achieve immortality in one leap. We will get there incrementally, with therapies that push the average lifespan to 100, then 120, then 150. This creates a society of the "chronologically confused." What does a 130-year-old with the body of a 40-year-old do? Are they still working? Are they on their fifth marriage? How does a society built on the assumption of a finite lifespan handle the economic and social burden of a population that simply doesn't retire? The In-Between State is a world of generational pile-ups, where multiple generations, each with different levels of enhancement, coexist in a state of profound social dislocation.

This creates a new, brutal form of inequality: "enhancement disparity." The wealthy will have access to the latest, most reliable, and most powerful augmentations. They will be smarter, healthier, and longer-lived. The rest of the population will be left with the buggy, off-brand, or older-generation tech. The gap between the "haves" and the "have-nots" will no longer be about money or opportunity, but about fundamental biology. We will have, in effect, created a new class system written into our very DNA. The "Gen-H" (genetically enhanced humans) will look upon the "Naturals" with a mixture of pity and contempt, seeing them as a throwback to a more primitive, fragile version of the species.

The psychological toll of the In-Between State will be immense. To be perpetually "in between" is to be in a state of constant anxiety and inadequacy. You will always be aware of the next upgrade you can't afford, the next therapy that could make you better, smarter, faster. The self will become a perpetual work-in-progress, a machine that is never fully optimized. The human condition will shift from one of acceptance of our natural limitations to one of relentless, technologically-driven self-improvement, a rat race where the finish line is always moving.

This will also be an age of profound identity crisis. What does it mean to be "human" when your personality can be tweaked with a software update to your neural implant? What is an "authentic" experience when your emotions can be modulated by a chemical dispenser synced to your calendar to ensure you are "happy" at your daughter's wedding? The In-Between State is a world where the very concept of a stable, authentic self becomes a luxury, a quaint notion for the un-enhanced.

We will see the rise of "enhancement subcultures." There will be the "Bio-purists," who reject all forms of augmentation, choosing to live as "Naturals" in a world that increasingly sees them as obsolete. There will be the "Synth-aesthetics," who embrace augmentation not for performance, but for art, turning their bodies into canvases for radical, non-functional cybernetic expression. There will be the "Memory Curators," who use BCIs to meticulously edit and craft their own life experiences, creating personalized, idealized versions of their past.

And there will be failures, catastrophic ones. For every successful enhancement, there will be countless stories of botched surgeries, of neural implants that are hacked, of genetic therapies that go horribly wrong. The In-Between State will be littered with the casualties of our evolutionary ambition, people who are left broken, disfigured, or insane by their attempts to transcend their humanity. These "glitched" individuals will be the ghosts of this new age, a constant, terrifying reminder of the risks of playing God.

The grand dream of transhumanism is a clean, elegant escape from the messiness of the human condition. The reality of the In-Between State will be the opposite. It will be a doubling-down on that messiness, an amplification of our anxieties, our inequalities, and our deepest insecurities. It’s the awkward teenage years of our species, complete with the painful growth spurts, the identity crises, and the terrible fashion choices of early-generation cybernetics.

We are not heading for a singular, unified post-human future. We are heading for a fractured, chaotic, and deeply unequal world of multiple human, and post-human, species living side-by-side. The In-Between State is not a transition. It may be the destination. Our mistake is to see transhumanism as a journey to a specific endpoint. It is more likely a permanent state of becoming, a relentless, open-ended process of technological self-modification with no final form. We may never arrive at the post-human. We may simply be doomed to be forever in between, a species perpetually caught between the animal we were and the god we want to be, never fully belonging to either world.
